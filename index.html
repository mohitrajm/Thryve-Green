India Has a Waste Problem and an Energy Problem

Every year, India produces millions of tons of crop residue and other biomass waste. A large portion of it is burned in the open or left unused. At the same time, many industries still depend heavily on coal and firewood for energy.

This results in more air pollution, wasted resources, and higher environmental and health costs. The briquette industry may be mature, but India’s waste challenge is still far from solved. The volume of agricultural residue produced each year leaves room for many responsible operators. We chose to start here because it is practical, proven, and builds the right foundation for the larger journey we want to create.

We Turn Waste Into Clean, Useful Fuel

Thryve Green takes biomass waste and converts it into high-quality briquettes that offer a cleaner alternative to traditional fuels.

Work With Farmers and FPOs

We source crop residue and biomass from farmers and FPOs, creating new income opportunities from material that often ends up burned.

Process Into Briquettes

Using efficient processes and machinery, we turn biomass into dense, uniform briquettes suitable for industrial use.

Supply to Industries

Industries use our briquettes to reduce emissions, improve ESG reporting, and move toward cleaner energy without major system changes.

Briquettes Are Just the Beginning

We started with briquettes because they solve a real problem today. They reduce coal use and help prevent open burning.

Our long-term goal is to create multiple waste-to-resource solutions for India. Our mission is to turn India’s waste into clean energy, materials, and fuels that reduce pollution and make sustainability effortless.
